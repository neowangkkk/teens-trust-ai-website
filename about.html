About the Project

From June – September 2025, 25 high school students from the LinkedKey Foundation's Leading Tomorrow program undertook an ambitious research project to understand how Canadian teenagers perceive the risks of artificial intelligence. Mentored by Professor Tao Wang, Faculty of Information, University of Toronto, the team designed and executed a mixed-methods study spanning 16 schools across the Greater Toronto Area.

Research Questions

  1. 1

    How do teenagers encounter and use AI in daily life?

  2. 2

    How do teenagers demonstrate understanding and critical awareness of AI systems?

  3. 3

    How do parents and educators influence teenagers' attitudes toward and usage of AI?

Methodology

Quantitative Survey

  • 156 responses collected
  • 16 schools across the GTA
  • 5-point Likert scale measuring risk perception across 4 categories
  • Demographics: gender, grade level, school type

Qualitative Interviews

  • 32 one-on-one interviews
  • Semi-structured format exploring personal AI experiences
  • Thematic analysis of attitudes toward data sharing, emotional use, and boundaries

Ethical Considerations

This research was conducted with careful attention to ethical standards. All participants provided informed consent, and for minors, parental consent was obtained. Survey responses were anonymous, and interview data was de-identified to protect participant privacy.

The research team received training on ethical research practices, including data handling, interviewing minors, and avoiding leading questions. The study design was reviewed by the faculty mentor to ensure alignment with academic research standards.
